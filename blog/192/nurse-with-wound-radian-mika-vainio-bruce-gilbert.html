13-15TH MAY

NETAUDIO

Nurse With Wound, Radian, Mika Vainio & Bruce Gilbert

Nurse With Wound

14-02-2011

15 May 2011, doors 7pm
Early Bird Tickets at £18+bf (until 20 March, limited availability, £22+bf adv thereafter)

Netaudio London is thrilled to present Nurse With Wound, Radian and a new live collaboration between Bruce Gilbert (ex-Wire) and Mika Vainio (ex Pan-Sonic) as the culmination of the 2011 Netaudio London festival.

The night is headlined by avant garde, surrealist Steve Stapleton’s Nurse With Wound project. A 30+ year career under the curatorial guide of post-industrial pioneer Steve Stapleton has seen Nurse With Wound collaborate with highly respected free thinkers including David Tibet (Current 93), William Bennett (Whitehouse) and Andrew McKenzie (Hafler Trio). NWW will perform a wide variety of improvised, outsider music in a genre defying experience, consistently returning to the touchstone of musique concrète. This rare occasion is not to be missed.

On the same bill, Netaudio presents an exclusively commissioned live collaboration by Mika Vainio (ex-Pan-Sonic) & Bruce Gilbert (ex-WIRE). Using a plethora of table top analog and digital equipment, this performance draws upon the creativity of two unique musical talents.

Mika Vainio’s solo work is known for its analog warmth and electronic harshness, finding a home on labels including Touch, Raster Noton and Mego. Bruce Gilbert is a founder member of punk/post-punk stalwarts Wire and the Mute Records outfit Dome. Wire’s minimal approach and unorthodox structures are revered as one of the most influential groups of the 70s & 80s.

Support is given by the Austrian based trio Radian, who have released two critically acclaimed albums on Thrill Jockey. Re-purposing and re-recording sound sources, they use a wide array of technology to create an arresting live performance. The resulting sonic palette has an acute attention to detail with texture and intensity that is unforgettable.
